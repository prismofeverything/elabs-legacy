What is Sol?

Players assume the role of of one of five factions, racing against time to gather resources needed to launch an Ark into interstellar space. Deploy sundivers from your orbiting mothership and use them to build bridgeheads deeper into the sun. Construct and position energy nodes, sundiver foundries, and transmission towers—or use your opponents’ for your own ends. Beam the energy you harvest to your Ark, imparting the momentum it will need to escape the coming supernova!

Sol is competitive, but not zero-sum. Often the best move is mutually beneficial, but Sol is neither a collaborative nor cooperative game. It’s a race game, where more aggressive gameplay speeds the clock, challenging players to leaven ambition with restraint. And it’s a [something something] with [cards that do things?].

Many Ways to Play

Sol’s primary play mode allows for 2–5 players, with a compelling solo play variant. The 96 instability and 30 instability effect cards ensure that every session is unique, and allows fine-tuning the game’s duration, degree of difficulty, and player-v-player aggressiveness.

Players can also choose to explore Sol as a cooperative “multiplayer-solo” game, to begin gameplay with “vestigial structures” already in place, or add trigger event cards for additional solar unpredictability. Learn more about the many ways to play Sol »

The Worlds of Sol

Sol is set in a richly-textured utopian future, a time of limitless resources in which war, poverty and injustice are distant memories…until the sun begins to die. The lushly illustrated Mythos Book, included with your copy of Sol, provides a window into the vibrant cultures competing to preserve their way of life in the face of impending solar annihilation. Which faction will you play? Will you launch your culture’s Ark in time?

A shared passion

Sol is the brainchild of brothers Ryan Spangler and Sean Spangler, some of whose earliest memories involve playing—and making—board games. Their shared passion for strategy, camaraderie, adrenaline, and the act of bringing something new into the world drives them to design the games that they want to play. We hope you’ll want to play them as well.

Sol is by all definitions an indy game, but it owes its existence to a vast supportive community of family, friends, and enthusiastic backers. We have so many people to thank.
